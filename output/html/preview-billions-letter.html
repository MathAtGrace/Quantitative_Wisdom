Worksheet 1.2.2 Preview Orders of Magnitude
A4US

Objectives

  • Estimate orders of magnitude

  • Calculate unit conversions

Use the above estimation strategy, together with an others you are familiar with, to decide if the following quantities are in the millions, billions, trillions, or some other size, such as tens of millions. (Note:} For some of these questions you could find the exact values, but for others, such as populations, any number would be an estimate.)

1.

The number of seconds you have been alive.

2.

The amount of money needed to send all adults in the United States to college for four years. Assume there are 200 million adults in the United States.

3.

The number of words in the Lord of the Rings trilogy.

Refine your above estimates by using a calculator and obtaining any missing information you need from the internet.

4.

The number of seconds you have been alive. For example, for a 10-year-old: \(60 \times 60 \times 24 \times 365 \times 10 = 315,360,000\) or about 315 million seconds.

5.

The amount of money needed to send all adults in the United States to college for four years. Estimate yearly tuition to be about 17,000. Again, assume there are about 200 million adults in the United States.

6.

The number of words in Lord of the Rings trilogy. Using an internet search, we found that a sample page of Lord of the Rings has about 300 words on it, and the series is 1,008 pages long.

You should be able to do the following things for the next class. Rate how confident you are on a scale of 1-5 (1 = not confident and 5 = very confident).
