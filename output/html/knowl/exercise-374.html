Exercise 10.

There is evidence that smiling can attenuate judgments of possible wrongdoing. This phenomenon termed the “smile-leniency effect” was the focus of a study by Marianne LaFrance & Marvin Hecht in 1995 52 . The following data are measurements of how lenient the sentences were for three different types of smiles and one neutral control. The same subject was used for all of the conditions so that may affect the results. The second column is a continuation of the first column.

  1. Find the standard deviation for each type of smile and the neutral control.

  2. Calculate the 5-number summary for type of smile and the neutral control.

  3. Calculate the range and IQR for each type of smile and the neutral control.

  4. Create side-by-side boxplots and compare and contrast the four groups.
