Item.

Multiplying something by the number 1 does not change its value. Example:
\begin{equation*} \frac{\$4.99}{2 \text{ pounds}} \cdot 1 = \frac{\$4.99}{2 \text{ pounds}} \end{equation*}
However, multiplying by the number 1 can be used to change the way a something appears. Example: to rewrite fifths as tenths:
\begin{equation*} \frac{4}{5} = \frac{4}{5} \cdot \frac{2}{2} = \frac{8}{10} \end{equation*}
